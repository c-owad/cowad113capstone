On a high level, I went about this process by giving AI (Gemini Pro) very specific prompts, often asking it to think carefully on a high-level
how it would go about implementing a feature, and then actually implement. This is a non-agentic approach, but one I found very helpful regardless.
I made this choice because it felt very intuitive for the relatively streamlined process of implementing one feature at a time, without having to 
worry about the cohesion of ideas or my desired outcomes for the project being relayed exactly properly across different AI models which is a barrier
of the agentic approach.
From my pre-113 approach, I think I often just asked an AI model to do
whatever it is what I wanted, often with large and difficult-to-digest amounts of work to implement all at once, additionally without having the model
do the aforementioned thoughtful planning beforehand, which I of course now know is far less effective than the strategy I employed, or any other
good strategy we came up with in 15-113.
If I had more time, I would want to implement some sort of leaderboard feature, so you can see how well your WPMs compare against other users
on the given mode (RPG/Standard). Of course this would be complicated to do as it would involve a database and perhaps a server of some sort.
